Natalie Pargas

My name is Natalie, I'm in the 11th grade, and I go to Basis DC. Ever since I was a child, I’ve assumed that STEM fields were for the mathematically gifted and not for me. It wasn’t until I got plopped down in a STEM-focused school that I realized challenging things can also be rewarding. The pride of being placed in a higher math class than my (male) cousin, who was previously assumed to be much better in math than me, sparked a fire in me. I could be one of those glorified, genius STEM people! So the next year when an opportunity to learn Java was made available to me, I took it. Around 75% of the kids had already tried these various programming languages while I was a complete newbie. However, I worked at it until I finally figured out how to switch into the programmer's mindset. It was history from there.
